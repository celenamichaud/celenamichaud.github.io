Welcome to the Website!

First of all, thanks for supporting our project.

This project's mission is to help guests and especially students with disabilities navigate the Athens campus at the University of Georgia. With the help and advice of the Disability Resource Center, we've designed a website prototype for a resource that can provide more qualitative information about campus itself and buildings on campus. Expansive future plans for the project include additional images with scaled viewing, a rating system, and on the report feature, an ability to pin a map location to better relay issue information to the DRC.

Our current goal for this website is to give an overview of the plans the website could carry out. We want to provide information about what buildings are best suited for students with disabilities. With this in mind, we've kept our layout relatively simple and straightforward so it's easier for people to understand and not feel overwhelmed. For example, the color theme of our page is meant to be high-contrast to help students who might face visual impairments. The fonts are also easy to read. The navigation bar does have dropdowns, but organization is concise.

Most importantly, if you are viewing on a desktop, we'd like to encourage you to view this website on a mobile device as well to see how we've modified that experience. Having a mobile-friendly website is crucial in this day and age, not only because of the abundance of mobile devices, but also because a mobile website is less discriminatory than offering an app. (Hello, Android users!).

Once again, thanks for taking the time to view our website. Feel free to look around, but note that buildings without a webpage will not load.
Pages we suggest: Snelling Commons, Myers Hall, Science Learning Center, & Science Library.

- Celena Michaud & Brian Nelson
